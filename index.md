# SoSy 2026
[4th Solid Symposium](https://sosy2026.eu/), London, 30 April & 1 May 2026

## Shaping the business value of trust and innovating the business models of Solid

Organisers: [Elfi Goesaert](https://www.linkedin.com/in/elfi-goesaert-35057557/) and [Bart Buelens](https://www.linkedin.com/in/bart-buelens-73962a2/), VITO, Belgium | [Jeremy Caine](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jeremycaine/), IBM, Australia.

### About this session

In the ever-changing ecosystem of data on the web, novel paradigms are developing on how to deal with data and its sovereignty, who can lay claims to data and how value creation is distributed.

The concept of data spaces is getting traction in Europe, setting up federated data architectures with data sovereignty at its core. It sets up trusted ecosystems that can be translated into value for both data holders and data users. Systems and paradigms are being developed in parallel to focus on the role of personal data spaces in these ecosystems. Solid provides the underlying architecture and basic philosophy to set up such a personal data space, but very little is made very concrete on how it can take up its role in the data economy.

In this session, we will look at successful cases of novel approaches to data sharing and data sovereignty, such as (personal) data spaces and how business value can be created from personal data stores. Generally, however, individuals lack control of their personal data locked in organisations' IT systems. Just as CGI was the killer enabling technology for the explosion of business on the web, there will be Solid based architectures that organisations can adopt to find new business value and trust.

The goal of this session is to explore the business models of personal data sovereignty that organisations will be willing to adopt and integrate into their operations.
